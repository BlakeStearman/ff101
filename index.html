What the heck is fantasy football?
You manage a small football team.
You play against another team each week of the football season.
You score points based on the performance of the players on your team that week.
What does a fantasy football team look like?
  • 1x Quarterback (QB)
  • 2x Running backs (RB)
  • 2x Wide Receivers (WR)
  • 1x Tight End (TE)
  • 2x Flex (RB / WR / TE)
  • 1x Kicker (K)
  • 1x Defense / Special Team (DST)
  • 5x Bench (BN)
  • 1x Injured Reserve (IR)
If you are familiar with football, most of these positions will be familiar…

…but what about the Flex position?

The flex position allows you to start a running back, wide receiver, or tight end.
What about the Defense / Special Teams position?

You play the defense and special teams for an entire team.

Injured reserve?

This position can be filled by a player who is injured, or is listed as out. The player can stay there as long as they are not eligible to play.

The bench slots hold the players you aren't actively playing that week.
How do you build your team?
A draft is held just before the football season begins.
A draft is run in rounds.

Each round of a draft, every person picks a player from the pool.

There are 15 rounds.

Every round the order reverses.

The last person to pick from a round, picks first in the following round.

This is called a snake draft.

Draft tips:

Don't draft players you really dislike.

Try not to overreach for a favorite player.

What is the waiver wire?

The waiver wire is the pool of all the players who aren't on a team.

These players are free agents.

So how does the waiver wire work?

Every week, free agents are put on waivers.

This is a temporary freeze on unclaimed players, giving everyone a chance to make a claim.

The waiver period lasts from start of Sunday games until Tuesday midnight, or for two days after the player has been dropped.

When waiver period ends, all claims are processed.

If two claims are for the same player, claim with highest waiver priority wins.

Waiver priority resets each week to inverse of standings. Last place player has highest priority.

Once waivers run, remaining unpicked players are again free agents.

Free agents can be added to your team at will, first come, first serve.

Runs from Wednesday morning until the beginning of the Sunday games.

What makes a good trade?
Identify…

Weaknesses of your team

What you're willing to trade.

Scout other teams for their weakness or needs.
Make an offer.

Make offers to multiple teams.

Trading tips:

Try to be equitable.

Don't trade like for like.
